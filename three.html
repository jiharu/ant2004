One of the classical goal of the drawing is to observe the three-dimensional form and translate it on a two-dimensional surface. > The process of sighting provides the method for making this translation easily and effectively. [link 1](http://www.jaimetreadwell.com/DCCC-Drawing-Contour-Line-Examples.htm), [link 2](http://drawingincollege.blogspot.kr/2013/01/sighting.html), [link 3](https://www.pinterest.com/pin/437341813787018459/), [link 4](https://drawing113.wordpress.com/tag/drawing-113-2/), [link 5](http://hoenartinsight.com/ceramics/introduction-to-drawing-fundamentals/) #### Making sighting stick & viewfinder - If the sighting stick is slender, it won't interfere with observing the form, however in the absence of a more suitable tool, a pencil will be an alternative. - Viewfinders are perceptual aids that will help you "frame" your view and compose your drawings. ![vf](img/03/vf.jpg) ______________ ## Space as the subject In all pictures, spaces between things are as important as the things themselves. ![magicmirror](img/m-c-escher-magic-mirror.jpg) M. C. Escher, lithograph, 1946 ### Negative spaces >In drawing, negative spaces are real. They are not just "the empty parts".. negative spaces in drawings are just as solid, just as important as the positive forms. For the person just learning to draw, they are perhaps more important, because negative spaces make drawing difficult things easy. ### Shared edges > The edges of the positive forms share edges with the negative spaces. If you draw one, you will have also drawn the other. ## Review II - Tumblr ___________ ### Assignments *Mar 18* : Preparation for next class (let's discuss about the class layout and structure) 1.
